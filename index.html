About Me

I am a Ph.D. student at University of Science and Technology of China (USTC), where I am fortunate to be advised by Professor Xiangnan He and Fuli Feng. Before that, I obtained a bachelor's degree in Electronic Information Engineering from USTC.

My research focuses on Human-in-the-Loop across fundamental machine learning tasks — developing efficient methods for high-quality sample selection and sample-efficient model training. Looking forward, I am especially interested in two directions. The first is data measure and selection for LLM post-training: designing principled criteria for curating diverse training signals — including long reasoning traces, preference pairs, and agentic trajectories in software engineering — to maximize post-training efficiency and generalization. The second is automatic data quality discovery: developing recursive analysis and validation frameworks that surface latent patterns distinguishing high- from low-quality samples, enabling scalable and self-improving data curation pipelines.

If you share common interests and would like to explore collaboration or simply have a discussion, feel free to contact me.

Internship Experience

  • Alibaba Tongyi Lab — Research Intern (Since July 2022)
    Built upon multi-view clustering techniques to mitigate the degradation in clustering precision caused by outlier samples. The resulting human-in-the-loop framework reduced both computational and wet-lab costs for active molecule discovery in molecular docking by two orders of magnitude.

  • Shanghai Qiji Zhifeng Co., Ltd. — Research Intern (Since March 2025)
    Conducted large-scale ablation studies on distilled data to investigate performance upper bounds under the knowledge distillation paradigm for mathematical reasoning. Improved PPO/GRPO training through redesigned advantage estimation and enhanced training stability. The resulting techniques were adopted in training the Nex-N2 model, which achieves leading open-source performance on agentic benchmarks.

  • Tencent CSIG, CodeBuddy/WorkBuddy Team — Research Intern (Since March 2026)
    Developed a composite data selection method for software engineering agent trajectories, jointly optimizing task difficulty, distributional diversity, and the extent of environment interaction. Achieved performance comparable to Kimi-Dev using less than one-tenth of the data, and investigated data scaling strategies for the mid-training of coding LLMs.
